## 4. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

### The Upsell Trigger

**Sequence that converted €2,500 ask → €3,500 purchase:**

1. She asked about €2,500 payment plan (testing affordability)
2. You confirmed split was possible ("galime be pabrangimo")
3. She immediately asked about the MORE expensive plan's split
4. You confirmed that too
5. She bought €3,500

**Insight:** Payment plan question was proxy for "can I afford the expensive one?" Confirming flexibility on the lower tier gave permission to desire the higher tier.

### Price Anchoring Gap

**What was missing:**
- No explicit "agency rate" comparison
- No "DIY cost" calculation
- She raised "too cheap" objection — sign of inadequate anchoring

**Recommended anchor table:**
```
| Service | Agency Rate | Our Rate |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Technical audit | €3,500 | Included |
| Keyword research | €1,500-3,000 | Included |
| 100 articles | €20,000+ | Included |
| Total value | €40,000+ | €2,500 |
```

### Payment Plan Structure

**What worked:**
- €2,500: 1000 + 1500 (40/60 split, 1 month)
- €3,500: 2 payments over 2 months (~1750 + 1750)
- "Be pabrangimo" (no markup) — critical trust signal

**Recommendation:** Pre-announce payment plans. Don't wait for customers to ask.
