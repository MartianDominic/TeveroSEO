**Status:** Extracted & Ready for Implementation

## Executive Summary

This document defines the absolute, non-negotiable writing rules for Phase 92 content generation. These rules enforce the "never generic, always specific" principle across all content generated by TeveroSEO, ensuring content that ranks AND gets cited by LLMs.

The rules are structured in three formats:
1. **Prompt Injection Snippets** - For LLM generation guidance
2. **Regex/Pattern Rules** - For deterministic auditing
3. **Scoring Rubric** - For quality gate enforcement

---

## 1. Universal Writing Rules

### 1.1 Evidence Density Rule

**Principle:** Statistics every 150-200 words minimum.

**Prompt Injection:**
```
EVIDENCE DENSITY REQUIREMENT:
- Include at least one verifiable statistic, number, or data point every 150-200 words
- Statistics must be specific: "73% increase" not "significant increase"
- Include source attribution: "[Source: Industry Report 2025]" or "{{VERIFY: claim | source}}"
- Acceptable evidence types: percentages, dollar amounts, time measurements, quantities, dates, study results
- Each 500-token chunk MUST contain at minimum 2 quantifiable data points
```

**Scoring Rubric:**
| Density | Score | Label |
|---------|-------|-------|
| ≤150 words/evidence | 100 | Excellent |
| 151-175 words/evidence | 90 | Good |
| 176-200 words/evidence | 80 | Pass |
| 201-250 words/evidence | 60 | Warning |
| >250 words/evidence | 40 | Fail |

---

### 1.2 Weasel Word Ban

**Principle:** No hedging language. Commit to claims or don't make them.

**Banned Words (ZERO TOLERANCE):**
```
may, might, could potentially, possibly, perhaps
some experts say, many believe, most people think
it is believed, it is thought, it is considered
relatively, fairly, somewhat, rather, quite
tends to, seems to, appears to, is said to
arguably, supposedly, allegedly, reportedly
in theory, in principle, generally speaking
for the most part, more or less, sort of, kind of
```

**Replacement Strategy:**
- Instead of "may help": "helps" (with evidence) or remove claim entirely
- Instead of "some experts say": "[Expert Name] at [Organization] states"
- Instead of "could potentially increase": "increases by X%" (with source)
- Instead of "relatively affordable": "$X per month" (specific number)

**Scoring:** 0 violations = 100, 1 = 85, 2 = 70, 3+ = 40 (Fail)

---

### 1.3 Specificity Over Vagueness

**Principle:** Specific numbers over vague quantities. Always.

**Banned Vague → Required Specific:**
- "many companies" → "73% of Fortune 500 companies [Source]"
- "costs a lot" → "costs $2,500-$5,000 per month"
- "saves time" → "saves 12 hours per week on average"
- "increases significantly" → "increases by 340%"
- "several years ago" → "in Q2 2022"
- "a large number" → "over 50,000 users"
- "highly effective" → "87% success rate in clinical trials"
- "industry-leading" → "#1 ranked by G2 with 4.9/5 stars"

---

### 1.4 Active Voice Preference

**Principle:** Active voice conveys authority. Passive voice signals uncertainty.

**Requirements:**
- Use active voice for 80%+ of sentences
- Passive voice permitted ONLY for scientific methodology or when actor is genuinely unknown

**Transformations:**
- "Results can be achieved" → "You achieve results"
- "Traffic is increased by SEO" → "SEO increases traffic"
- "Content was created" → "Our team created content"
- "It is recommended that" → "We recommend"

---

### 1.5 No Content Fluffing

**Principle:** Every word earns its place. No filler.

**DELETE sentences that:**
- Repeat what was just said in different words
- State obvious truths ("SEO is important for visibility")
- Use multiple adjectives where one would suffice
- Add transitional padding ("Now let's move on to discuss...")

**Fluff Phrases to Avoid:**
```
"It goes without saying that..."
"Needless to say..."
"As you probably know..."
"In other words..."
"Simply put..."
"At the end of the day..."
"The fact of the matter is..."
"All things considered..."
```

## 2. Structural Writing Rules

### 2.1 Keyword Placement

**Requirements:**
1. Primary keyword appears in first 100 words (ideally first 50)
2. Primary keyword in H1 (title)
3. Primary keyword in first paragraph
4. DO NOT stuff keywords in H2/H3/H4 (anti-optimization signal)
5. Use semantic variants naturally throughout

**Keyword Density:** Must be < 2.5%

---

### 2.2 Answer-First Structure

**Principle:** Answer the query in the first paragraph. No preamble.

**NEVER start with:**
- Definitions ("SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization...")
- History ("For decades, businesses have relied on...")
- General statements ("In today's digital landscape...")
- Questions back to reader ("Have you ever wondered...")

**ALWAYS start with:**
- The direct answer
- The key takeaway
- The most important number/fact
- The bottom-line recommendation

**Example for "best CRM for small business":**
- WRONG: "Customer Relationship Management software has become essential for businesses of all sizes. In this guide, we'll explore..."
- RIGHT: "HubSpot CRM is the best choice for most small businesses—free tier handles up to 1 million contacts, and paid plans start at $45/month. Here's why, and when alternatives like Pipedrive or Salesforce make more sense."

---

### 2.3 One Idea Per Paragraph

**Requirements:**
1. Each paragraph has ONE main point
2. Be 2-4 sentences (50-150 words for web)
3. Be self-contained (readable in isolation)

**Structure:**
- Sentence 1: Topic sentence (main point)
- Sentences 2-3: Supporting evidence or explanation
- Sentence 4 (if needed): Transition or conclusion

---

### 2.4 Section Transitions

**Requirements:**
- Each H2 section must end with a natural bridge to the next, OR
- The next H2 must open with clear context

**Good Transitions:**
- "With pricing understood, let's examine which option fits your situation best."
- "Now that you know the costs, here's how to actually book."
- Opening next section: "Beyond cost, location matters—here's how each lot compares for terminal access."

---

## 3. Evidence Rules

### 3.1 Claim-Proof Pairing

**Principle:** Every claim needs backing. No orphan assertions.

**Claim Types Requiring Proof:**
1. Statistical claims ("X% of companies...")
2. Comparative claims ("better than", "faster than")
3. Causal claims ("leads to", "causes", "results in")
4. Best/worst claims ("best practice", "top performer")
5. Future predictions ("will increase", "expected to grow")

**Proof Formats:**
- Source citation: "...according to [Source Name, Year]"
- Data reference: "...based on analysis of 10,000 websites"
- Study reference: "...in a study published by [Publisher]"
- Verification tag: "{{VERIFY: claim | specific source to check}}"

**VIOLATION:** Claim in paragraph 1, proof in paragraph 3 = NO. Must be same paragraph.

---

### 3.2 Expert Quote Format

**Every quote must include:**
1. Full name of the person
2. Title/role
3. Organization (verifiable)
4. Optional: credentials or context

**Format:**
"[Quote text]" — [Full Name], [Title] at [Organization]

**Example:**
GOOD: "Core Web Vitals directly impact ranking signals," says Martin Splitt, Developer Advocate at Google.
BAD: "Industry experts agree that SEO is important." (No attribution)

---

### 3.3 Source Quality Requirements

**Tier 1 (PREFERRED):**
- Official sources (government, standards bodies)
- Peer-reviewed research
- Primary data sources (company reports, SEC filings)
- Industry authorities (Google Webmaster Blog, W3C)

**Tier 2 (ACCEPTABLE):**
- Major industry publications (Search Engine Journal, Moz, Ahrefs)
- Reputable news outlets
- Academic institutions
- Professional associations

**NEVER CITE:**
- Content farms (generic listicle sites)
- Anonymous sources
- Circular citations
- Outdated content (>2 years for fast-moving topics)

---

## 4. Engagement Rules

### 4.1 First-Sentence Hook

**First sentence must do ONE of:**
1. STARTLE: Counterintuitive fact or surprising statistic
2. PROMISE: Clear benefit statement with specific outcome
3. IDENTIFY: Name the reader's exact pain point
4. ANSWER: Directly answer the query (for informational)

**Hook Examples:**
- STARTLE: "Google processes 8.5 billion searches daily, yet 90% of pages get zero organic traffic."
- PROMISE: "This 12-step audit catches the SEO issues costing you rankings—takes 45 minutes."
- IDENTIFY: "You've published 50 blog posts and traffic is flat. Here's what's actually broken."
- ANSWER: "HubSpot CRM—free tier, 1M contact limit, paid starts at $45/month."

**NEVER OPEN WITH:**
- "Welcome to our guide..."
- "In this article, we will..."
- "Have you ever wondered..."
- "[Topic] is important because..."

---

### 4.2 Value Proposition Placement

**By the third sentence, the reader must know:**
1. WHAT they will learn/gain
2. WHY this content specifically (not generic)
3. WHO this is for (implicit or explicit)

---

### 4.3 Scanability Requirements

**Headers:**
- H2 every 300-500 words maximum
- H2s must be descriptive (not "Introduction", "Conclusion")
- H2s should answer questions or promise value

**Visual Breaks:**
- Bullet list or table every 500 words minimum
- Bold key terms and takeaways
- Pull quotes or callouts for key insights

**Paragraph Length:**
- 2-4 sentences for web content
- No paragraph over 150 words
- White space between all paragraphs

---

## 5. Anti-Pattern Detection

### 5.1 Banned Opening Phrases (INSTANT FAIL)

**Definitional Openings:**
- "[Topic] is a [category] that..."
- "[Acronym] stands for..."
- "The definition of [topic] is..."
- "[Topic] refers to..."

**Time-Based Openings:**
- "In today's [fast-paced/digital/modern/competitive] world..."
- "In the [current/modern/digital] era..."
- "Throughout history..."
- "Since the dawn of..."
- "In recent years..."

**False Engagement:**
- "Have you ever wondered..."
- "Did you know that..."
- "Picture this:..."
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "Let me ask you a question..."

**Promise Openings:**
- "In this [article/guide/post], we will..."
- "This [comprehensive/ultimate/complete] guide..."
- "Everything you need to know about..."
- "The complete guide to..."

**Filler Openings:**
- "It goes without saying..."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "As we all know..."
- "Needless to say..."

---

### 5.2 AI-Slop Indicators (Reddit Test Failures)

**Overused AI Phrases (Replace with alternatives):**
| AI Phrase | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| dive into | examine, explore, analyze |
| landscape | market, industry, field |
| leverage | use, apply, employ |
| unlock | enable, gain, achieve |
| game-changer | significant improvement, breakthrough |
| robust | strong, comprehensive, thorough |
| cutting-edge | modern, latest, advanced |
| streamline | simplify, optimize, improve |
| synergy | collaboration, combination |
| holistic | comprehensive, complete |
| seamless | smooth, integrated |
| empower | enable, help |
| utilize | use |
| nestled | (BANNED completely) |

**Empty Authority Claims (require proof):**
- "industry-leading" → need ranking/award evidence
- "world-class" → need comparison data
- "best-in-class" → need benchmark
- "state-of-the-art" → need technical specification
- "revolutionary" → need measurable impact

**Non-Specific Value Claims (need numbers):**
- "save time and money" → "saves 12 hours/week and $500/month"
- "improve efficiency" → "improves efficiency by 34%"
- "boost productivity" → "boosts productivity 2.3x"
- "increase ROI" → "increases ROI from 150% to 340%"

---

### 5.3 Over-Optimization Patterns

**Keyword Stuffing:**
- Same exact keyword > 3 times per 500 words = FAIL
- Keyword in every H2/H3 = FAIL
- Keyword forced unnaturally = FAIL

**Link Stuffing:**
- More than 3 links in first 100 words = WARNING
- Links on every heading = FAIL
- Exact-match anchor text on every link = FAIL

**For LLM content generation, inject this master template:**

```
=== SEO-AGI WRITING RULES (ABSOLUTE - NON-NEGOTIABLE) ===

EVIDENCE DENSITY:
- Include verifiable statistic/number every 150-200 words
- Use {{VERIFY: claim | source}} for claims needing verification
- Every 500-token chunk needs minimum 2 quantifiable data points

WEASEL WORD BAN (ZERO TOLERANCE):
BANNED: may, might, could potentially, possibly, some experts say, many believe, relatively, fairly, somewhat, tends to, seems to, appears to
If you cannot prove it with evidence, do not write it.

SPECIFICITY:
Replace ALL vague quantities with specific numbers.
"many companies" → "73% of Fortune 500 companies"
"saves time" → "saves 12 hours per week"
"increases significantly" → "increases by 340%"

ACTIVE VOICE:
80%+ of sentences must be active voice.
"Results can be achieved" → "You achieve results"

NO FLUFF:
Every sentence must provide new information, advance argument, or give actionable guidance.
DELETE: "It goes without saying", "Needless to say", "As you probably know"

STRUCTURE:
- Primary keyword in first 100 words (ideally first 50)
- Answer the query directly in first 2-3 sentences
- NO preamble, NO definitions, NO "In this guide we will..."
- One idea per paragraph (50-150 words max)
- H2 every 300-500 words

EVIDENCE RULES:
- Every claim immediately backed by proof in same paragraph
- Expert quotes: "Quote" — Full Name, Title at Organization
- Cite Tier 1 sources (official, peer-reviewed, industry authority)

HOOKS:
First sentence must: STARTLE (counterintuitive fact), PROMISE (clear benefit), IDENTIFY (pain point), or ANSWER (direct response)
NEVER: "Welcome to...", "In this article...", "Have you ever wondered..."

BANNED OPENING PHRASES (INSTANT FAIL):
- "In today's [fast-paced/digital/modern] world..."
- "[Topic] is a [category] that..."
- "Have you ever wondered..."
- "This comprehensive guide will..."

AI-SLOP TO AVOID:
- dive into, landscape, leverage, unlock, game-changer, robust, cutting-edge, streamline, synergy, holistic
- "save time and money" (without numbers)
- "industry-leading" (without proof)
- Multiple superlatives in one sentence

OVER-OPTIMIZATION LIMITS:
- Keyword density < 2.5%
- Keyword in max 1 subheading
- Max 2 links in first 100 words

Quality gate requires 80+ score. Blockers prevent auto-publish regardless of score.
===
```

## 7. Vertical-Specific Rule Overrides

### 7.1 YMYL Verticals (Healthcare, Legal, Financial)

**Additional Requirements:**
- Expert reviewer attribution required
- Disclaimers mandatory ("This is not medical/legal/financial advice")
- Citations to .gov/.edu sources preferred
- No outcome guarantees

### 7.2 E-commerce

**Additional Requirements:**
- Price specificity mandatory
- Stock/availability signals
- Shipping cost/timeline transparency
- Product schema complete

### 7.3 Local/Service

**Additional Requirements:**
- NAP information complete
- Service area explicit
- Licensing/certification verification
- LocalBusiness schema required

## 8. Quality Gate Integration

### Composite Scoring Weights

| Category | Weight | Components |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Universal Rules | 40% | Evidence density (10%), Weasel words (10%), Specificity (10%), Active voice (5%), No fluff (5%) |
| Structural Rules | 25% | Keyword placement (8%), Answer-first (7%), Paragraph structure (5%), Transitions (5%) |
| Evidence Rules | 20% | Claim-proof pairing (10%), Expert quotes (5%), Source quality (5%) |
| Engagement Rules | 10% | Hook (4%), Value proposition (3%), Scanability (3%) |
| Anti-Pattern | 5% | Banned intros (BLOCKER), Filler (2%), AI-slop (2%), Over-optimization (1%) |

### Blockers (Fail Regardless of Score)

1. Any banned intro phrase detected
2. Excessive weasel words (3+)
3. Evidence density > 300 words/evidence
4. Keyword density > 2.5%

### Pass Threshold

- Overall score ≥ 80
- Zero blockers
- Auto-publish eligible only if score ≥ 80 AND no blockers
